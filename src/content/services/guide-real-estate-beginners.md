## Introduction

Real estate investing is a way to make money by buying and selling real estate. This guide will explain the fundamentals of real estate investing, and how beginning investors can get started.

## What is Real Estate Investing?

Real estate investing is a way to make money by buying and selling real estate. It can be done by individuals, businesses, or between a group of individuals. These purchases can be used strictly as an investment or for personal use as well.

These investments can be made for short term or long term gains: Short-term goals may include buying low and selling high; while longer-term goals might involve building equity over time while renting out property or creating a consistent income stream by adding and holding rental properties.

Real estate can be a good addition to a well rounded portfolio and can help you reach your retirement goals earlier.

Real estate has been one of the most lucrative investment opportunities for decades, but it's not for everyone. If you're just starting out, it's important to know that investing in real estate will require time and patience—especially if you're just getting started. If this sounds like something that would work best for someone who already has some experience learning about finance or investing, then keep reading!

## What to Ask Yourself Before Getting Into Real Estate

Before you put your money down on a rental property, take some time to think about the following questions:

**Time:** What is your time horizon? Do you want to invest for just a few years or do you have a long-term investment plan in mind? This will dictate what kind of investment you can or should make.

**Personal:** How much time can you devote to managing your investment? If it's just going to be part of a portfolio, maybe that's fine. But if this is something that requires constant attention and effort from day-to-day (or even hour-to-hour), consider whether it will fit into your schedule well enough.

**Funding:** What is your capital situation? Do you have the cash to purchase this yourself? Do you need funding? Where will you get that funding?

**Involvement:** Are you going to be active in every aspect of the process? This can include, but not limited to: finding properties, maintenance and repairs (self or contracted), negotiating sales and tenant management.

## Real Estate Investments & Property Types

There are many different types of real estate investments, but some common ones include:

### Rental Properties

Rental properties are the most common type of real estate investment. They can be single family homes, multi family homes, apartments and even commercial properties.

Rental properties are good for investors who want to build a portfolio of properties that they can sell or rent out at different times in their life cycle. It's also an excellent way to get your feet wet with real estate investing before committing more money into purchasing a home or condo outright.

Real estate rentals often come with maintenance costs associated with them so you have to make sure you understand what those costs are before making a purchase decision on one!

### Flipping Homes

Flipping houses is a great way to make money and get into real estate. Before you start flipping homes, it's important to know what you are getting yourself into.

Flipping houses means buying a property and then selling it for more than you paid for it. The goal is to make more money from the sale of your home than what you spent on purchasing it in the first place! If done correctly, this form of investing can be very lucrative over time as well as fun!

There are many factors involved in successfully flipping houses but here are some basic tips:

### Commercial Real Estate

Commercial real estate investing, or the investment of commercial property such as office buildings, apartments, condominiums and other structures that are used for business purposes. The best way to think about commercial real estate investing is as a long-term investment strategy: you're looking for a place that will appreciate in value over time, so that when you sell it, you'll have made money on top of what you paid for it. There are more than 4 million commercial properties across the country.

Office buildings are generally the most expensive type of commercial real estate investment. They are typically used for businesses that require high-traffic areas with ample parking or easy access to public transportation.

Apartments and condominiums are less expensive than office buildings, but still require significant amounts of money up front to purchase them.

You can also invest in land banking by purchasing undeveloped land. This means that developers plan on building something on it later on down the road such as an apartment building or shopping center.

### REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)

Real Estate Investment Trusts are corporations or trusts that invest in real estate by owning, managing and leasing various properties. Generally, REITs are passive investment vehicles. REITs are purchased on exchanges like the New York Stock Exchange or NASDAQ. REITs are good for investors looking for a consistent income as they pay out 90-100% of their taxable income to shareholders in the form of a dividend.

### Real Estate ETFs and Mutual Funds

Real estate ETFs and mutual funds are a collection of stocks, bonds or other securities that trade like a stock. This can allow an investor to invest in real estate for a lot less up front capital. These are generally longer term investments. Some ETFs and mutual funds are more volatile than others, so it's important to do your research before investing with these products. Mutual funds can tend to have larger expense ratios than ETFs.

## How to Find Quality Investment Opportunities

The first step in finding a rental property is to evaluate the market and decide what kind of investment you want to make. These investments should align with your short-term and long-term financial goals.

### Quick Flip

If you're looking for a quick flip, it's important to know that there are certain things that will make your house more attractive as an investment. For example, if the property has been in foreclosure, is undervalued and in an up and coming area; this can be considered an opportunity.

### Rentals

When investing in rental properties an important factor when deciding whether or not this investment will work out is location—whether or not there are enough renters nearby who would rent your property. Is the property zoned for a highly rated school, near a source of jobs or entertainment? The locality can help drive demand for your property and also lead to higher rental rates due to demand.

### General Considerations

A key question to ask is if this property has been sold recently? If so, did the property stay listed for a long time? Did those sales go smoothly without too many problems popping up along the way? This information helps identify potential risks associated with owning real estate investments before knowing whether or not these factors outweigh any advantages gained.

## How Do Recessions or Inflation Impact Real Estate?

Real estate investing, just like other investment means, can be affected by inflation or recessions. Real estate typically will perform better than assets such as stocks or bonds which can be heavily impacted by market volatility. For example, during the 2008 recession the housing market dropped 20%, which was the main cause of the crash, while the S&P 500 fell nearly 57% from its peak.

### Inflation

- Not the time to invest as interest rates rise
- This can be a good time if you own real estate as you could become the bank
- More difficult for consumers to purchase their own properties

### Recession

- Can negatively impact property values
- Real estate generally outperforms stocks/bonds
- Purchasing power during a recession is lower because generally governments will increase money supply which devalues the currency
- Consumers may not have the capital to buy
- Tenants could be laid off or furloughed impacting their ability to pay rent
- During recessions interest rates could be lowered which can provide opportunity to buy properties at a discount
- If interest rates stay low and the recession dissipates then you can see home values climb rapidly, making it a good time to sell inventory for cash flow

## How to Get Funding for Your Investment

In order to invest in real estate rentals you will need some form of funding. There are several ways that you can get this money:

### Conventional Financing

This is the typical loan provided by banks and credit unions to purchase a home an individual would reside in.

### FHA Financing

This loan type requires less down payment and lower minimum credit scores. These loans are backed by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA) and are used for a primary residence, not a rental. You could use this loan type to live in while renting out a room, or if it is a multi-family unit such as a duplex then the other part of the property.

### FHA 203K Loan

This is a similar loan to above but is used to rehab a home or for upgrades and improvements.

### SDIRAs for Real Estate

Self-directed IRAs can also be used to invest into real estate as they allow a variety of other investments, such as real estate. You could roll your 401K into an SDIRA and use it to invest.

### Securities-Based Lines of Credit

If you have a portfolio, such as a brokerage account, you can find loans that allow you to borrow against the value of that portfolio. The general rule of thumb is that most lenders will allow you to borrow up to 70% of the value of the loan. For example if you have a brokerage account with $100,000 worth of stock, you are able to borrow $70,000.

### Margin Loans

You can also take loans against the value of eligible stocks, bonds, mutual funds and exchange-traded funds, similar to securities-based lines of credit.

### VA Financing

Available to service members, surviving spouses or veterans. A portion of these loans are backed by the VA. These loans do not require a down payment or PMI (private mortgage insurance).

### Home Equity Loans (HELOCs)

A home equity line of credit is a loan which you take out equity out of a property that already exists. Generally the maximum amount of equity you are able to borrow is 80% of the value of the property. For example if a home is worth $500,000 and the mortgage remaining is $300,000 then you could borrow an additional $100,000 ($500,000 × 80% = $400,000. $400,000 - $300,000 = $100,000 available to borrow).

### Blanket Mortgage

A blanket mortgage is a loan where multiple properties are involved. The multiple properties in the loan are used as collateral for the entire loan.

### Private Money Lenders

Private lenders could be businesses or investors who can loan you money to buy property. These individuals have the capital to invest but may not want to be a part of the operations of buying, selling and rehabilitation.

### Hard Money Lenders

Hard money lenders provide short-term loans, generally with higher interest rates. These are usually used to bridge the gap. These loans are used when someone needs a loan in a short period as they expect to generate income quickly from the property or have other financing lined up but want to move quickly.

### Portfolio Loans

Portfolio loans are loans where the originator keeps the loan instead of selling it to a third party. This allows the lender to set their own terms instead of adhering to the Freddie Mac & Fannie Mae standards.

## Should You Use a Real Estate Agent?

Realtors are trained professionals who have access to a lot of information and resources. They know all the different neighborhoods, what they are like now and where investments should be considered. They can tell you if there are any foreclosures or high-risk areas in the area and what you would pay for insurance or repairs.

Experienced real estate professionals have connections within the community that can help them find properties for their clients with the focus on your specific needs and price range. Once the property is found they can handle most of the paperwork, negotiating and communication which can save you time and money.

### The Services a Realtor Provides to an Investor

- Finding properties for sale in your area
- Finding out about the history of a property and any issues with it (like if it's on a floodplain, it contains asbestos, etc.)
- Negotiating with owners on price and terms
- Working with contractors on getting repairs done or upgrades made to the property
- Connecting you to quality service providers, such as inspectors, to ensure any issue is spotted and fixed or discounted before purchased

You can also learn from others through your local real estate investors' association or group. This group can help you learn about the local community and what properties are available in that area so that you can find something that will work for you. They also can help connect you with other investors in your area, which will help you meet people who are interested in investing together.

Real estate agents have relationships with banks, mortgage companies, title companies—all sorts of people who can help investors get their money off the ground quickly and efficiently.
