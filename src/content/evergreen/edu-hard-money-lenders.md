## What Are Hard Money Lenders?

Hard money lending is a form of financing that's used by borrowers who need money quickly. It's also known for being a little more expensive than other types of loans, but it has its benefits.

Hard money lenders typically charge higher interest rates than traditional banks do because they don't have to go through as much red tape or paperwork when making their loans. These lenders will often lend up to 70% LTV (loan-to-value) which means that if you want $100K in financing and your home is worth $200K then you'll only need 20% down payment on top of whatever fees are associated with getting this type of loan approved.

## Criteria for Choosing a Hard Money Lending Company

### Experience and Track Record

The most important thing to look at when choosing a hard money lender is their experience. A good company will have been in business for several years, with an established reputation and track record of successful loans. They should also be able to show you their portfolio of past projects so that you can see how they've helped other businesses succeed.

### Interest Rates and Fees

While interest rates vary from lender to lender, they're typically between 10% - 20%, depending on your credit score and the amount being borrowed (the higher the riskier). Fees are another cost factor that must be considered; these may include:

- Application fees
- Origination fees (paid directly to the lender)
- Closing costs
- Points (an upfront payment made by borrowers)

### Loan Terms & Repayment Options

Most hard money loans offer repayment terms ranging from six months up until one year with monthly payments due every month during this period until all principal plus interest has been paid off in full at which point any remaining balance becomes due immediately upon demand by either party without further notice or notification being given beforehand whatsoever!

## Typical Hard Money Loan Terms

| Factor | Typical Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Interest Rate | 10% - 20% |
| LTV (Loan-to-Value) | Up to 70% |

## Steps to Apply for a Hard Money Loan

### Step 1: Determine Eligibility

The first step to applying for a hard money loan is to determine whether or not you're eligible. Most lenders will require that you have some sort of collateral and income, but there are also other factors that can affect your eligibility.

### Step 2: Gather Documentation

For example, if you're self-employed or have recently changed jobs, the lender may ask for:

- Proof of income from previous employers
- Tax returns from the last three years
- Documentation showing you own valuable property (such as real estate) to secure the loan

### Step 3: Submit Application

Complete the lender's application process with all required documentation.

### Step 4: Property Evaluation

The lender will evaluate the collateral property to determine loan amount and terms.

### Step 5: Approval and Funding

If approved, funds can be disbursed quickly—often within days.

## Risks and Benefits of Using a Hard Money Lending Company

### Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Quick Access to Funds** | Approval in days, not weeks or months |
| **Flexible Terms** | More negotiable than traditional banks |
| **Credit Flexibility** | Less emphasis on credit score |
| **Collateral-Based** | Loan based on property value, not just borrower profile |

### Risks

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Higher Interest Rates** | 10-20% vs. 4-8% for traditional loans |
| **Shorter Terms** | 6-12 months vs. 15-30 years |
| **Collateral Risk** | Property at risk if you default |
| **Higher Fees** | Origination, points, and other costs |

**Important:** Hard money lenders charge higher interest rates than traditional banks or credit unions do because they take on more risk when lending you money. They do this by requiring you to put up collateral for your loan (e.g., real estate) which allows them to recoup their investment if you default on your payments or fail to pay back the full amount borrowed at any point during the term of your agreement with them.

## Key Questions to Ask a Hard Money Lender

Hard money lending is a great way to get your hands on cash for real estate investing. It's important to consider the following when choosing a hard money lending company:

### About Loan Terms

- What are the terms of their loans?
- Will they charge you high interest rates?
- What is the loan-to-value ratio they offer?

### About Requirements

- How much equity do you need to put down on your property?
- Do they require any additional collateral or documentation?
- Do they require personal guarantees?
- Is income verification required (e.g., tax returns)?

### About Timeline

- How long will it take them to approve your loan request?
- What happens if they don't approve it in time for closing on your deal?
- What is the typical funding timeline after approval?

## When to Consider Hard Money

Hard money loans are best suited for:

- **Real estate investors** needing quick closings
- **Fix and flip projects** with short timelines
- **Borrowers with credit issues** who can't qualify for traditional financing
- **Time-sensitive opportunities** where speed matters more than cost
- **Bridge financing** while waiting for permanent financing

## Conclusion

Hard money lending provides fast, flexible financing for real estate investors and borrowers who need quick access to capital. While the higher interest rates and shorter terms make it more expensive than traditional financing, the speed and flexibility can be invaluable for the right projects.

### Key Takeaways

- Interest rates typically 10-20%
- Loan terms 6-12 months
- Up to 70% LTV with 20%+ down payment
- Approval in days, not weeks
- Best for time-sensitive real estate investments
- Higher risk requires careful project evaluation

Before choosing a hard money lender, carefully evaluate your project's timeline, budget, and exit strategy to ensure this type of financing aligns with your investment goals.
