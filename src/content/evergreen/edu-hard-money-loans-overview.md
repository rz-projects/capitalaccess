## What Is a Hard Money Loan?

Hard money loans are a type of loan that is backed by real estate for collateral. Because they are outside of the traditional lending system, that means they don't have to be approved by a bank or other lender before they can be used.

Hard money loans are great for short-term financing needs and they typically come with higher interest rates than other types of loans because they're much riskier than getting approval from a federally insured bank or credit union. These loans saw great expansion after the 2008 recession mainly due to the Dodd-Frank Act which put more regulations on banks and lenders.

## Ways to Use a Hard Money Loan

### Fix & Flip / Bridge

Obtain capital to repair or flip a home. You can also use the capital to bridge the gap until another transaction is complete.

### Rentals

This loan type will allow individuals to enter or obtain new rental properties to their portfolio.

### Commercial

If a traditional loan is insufficient or you are unable to secure one then a hard money loan can allow you to build new or access funds for owner-occupied.

### Transactional

Wholesalers and brokers can use transactional funding to buy and sell properties quickly.

## Hard Money Loan Uses Summary

## Pros & Cons of Hard Money Loans

### Pros

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fast Approval Process** | Less requirements than traditional loans leads to quicker approval, funding within days to weeks |
| **Based on Collateral, Not Credit** | No credit checks or income verification makes application easier |
| **Flexible Terms** | Lenders work with borrowers individually to create workable terms |
| **Quick Access to Capital** | Can close deals that require fast action |
| **Property-Based Approval** | Approval based on asset value, not borrower profile |

### Cons

| Disadvantage | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Higher Rates** | Interest rates can be as high as 25% per year, origination fees typically 2-3% |
| **Lower LTV Ratio** | 50-80% LTV vs. 75-80% for traditional mortgages |
| **Shorter Terms** | Typically 6-24 months vs. 15-30 years |
| **Higher Down Payment** | May need 20-50% down depending on LTV |
| **Restrictions/Regulations** | Some lenders won't do owner-occupied; state/federal laws may apply |

## Hard Money vs Traditional Loans

## What Is the Process of Getting a Loan?

Hard money loans follow a similar process as traditional loans. The main difference in process is the timeline from start to funding is generally within days whereas a traditional loan can take 30 days or longer.

### Step-by-Step Process

| Step | Description | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **1. Find Property** | Identify property to purchase | Varies |
| **2. Find Lender** | Research reputable hard money lenders | 1-3 days |
| **3. Pre-Qualification** | Lender evaluates borrower and property | 1-2 days |
| **4. Loan Application** | Submit formal application | 1 day |
| **5. Processing & Underwriting** | Lender reviews documentation | 2-5 days |
| **6. Approval & Closing** | Final approval and funding | 1-3 days |

**Total Timeline:** Typically 7-14 days from start to funding

## Choosing a Hard Money Lender

When selecting a hard money lender it is important to consider several factors to ensure you get the best loan to help you achieve your purchasing goals.

### Key Factors to Consider

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Years in Business** | An experienced lender will provide invaluable support and potentially better rates |
| **LTV Calculation** | Understand how they calculate LTV (purchase price vs. after repair value) |
| **Location** | Some lenders only do deals in the state they are located |
| **Loan Requirements** | Each company can have different repayment options |
| **Property Type Expertise** | Some lenders prefer specific property types |
| **Interest Rates & Fees** | Compare rates and fee structures across lenders |
| **Reputation** | Check reviews, references, and track record |

### Questions to Ask Potential Lenders

- How many years have you been in business?
- What is your typical LTV ratio?
- What are your interest rates and origination fees?
- What property types do you specialize in?
- What states do you lend in?
- What is your typical approval timeline?
- Do you require a personal guarantee?
- What are your prepayment penalties?

## FAQs About Hard Money Loans

### Who is Eligible For a Hard Money Loan?

Anyone is eligible for a hard money loan. There are no credit requirements and the property's value is used to secure the loan. Hard money lenders like to see borrowers with at least 2 years experience as real estate investors before they will consider providing someone with a hard money loan because they will trust that the investor has a track record of success and can pay them back without issue.

### What is the Process for a Hard Money Loan?

After finding a reputable company you can start the application process. The lender will view your loan amount and compare that to the value and equity within the collateral. If they deem the loan viable, they will proceed with underwriting and approval.

### How are Hard Money Loans Different Than Traditional Loans?

Hard money loans have less requirements than traditional loans. They are also provided by individuals, private equity or investment funds compared to banks or credit unions. They are also a much faster option than traditional loans which can allow you to buy a property quickly.

### What Interest Rates Can I Expect?

| Rate Type | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Interest Rate** | 10% - 25% annually |
| **Origination Fee** | 2% - 3% of loan amount |
| **Points** | 1 - 4 points |

### What Documents Are Typically Required?

- Purchase agreement or contract
- Property information and address
- Scope of work (for renovations)
- Budget for repairs
- Exit strategy
- Proof of funds for down payment
- Previous investment experience (if applicable)

## Conclusion

Hard money loans provide a valuable financing option for real estate investors who need quick access to capital or don't qualify for traditional financing. While they come with higher costs and shorter terms, their speed and flexibility make them essential tools for many investment strategies.

### Key Takeaways

- Hard money loans are collateral-based, not credit-based
- Faster approval and funding than traditional loans
- Higher interest rates (10-25%) reflect the speed and risk
- Best for short-term investment strategies
- Choose lenders based on experience, rates, and property expertise
- Have a clear exit strategy before borrowing

### When to Consider Hard Money

- Need to close quickly on a property
- Don't qualify for traditional financing
- Property needs significant repairs
- Short-term investment strategy (fix and flip)
- Bridge financing needed between transactions
